Week 1:

Read "TED Talks: The Official TED Guide to Public Speaking" by Chris Anderson to gain insights into effective public speaking.
Watch TED Talks and analyze the speakers' presentation styles and techniques.
Practice delivering short individual presentations on various topics, focusing on clarity and organization.

Week 2:

Read "Slide:ology: The Art and Science of Creating Great Presentations" by Nancy Duarte to understand principles of effective slide design.
Explore online resources and tutorials on creating visually appealing and engaging presentations.
Create sample presentations using visual aids and practice delivering them.

Week 3:

Read "The Silent Language of Leaders: How Body Language Can Help--or Hurt--How You Lead" by Carol Kinsey Goman to learn about the importance of body language in presentations.
Observe and analyze the body language of effective speakers in videos or live presentations.
Practice delivering presentations while consciously incorporating appropriate body language techniques.

Week 4:

Read "Crucial Conversations: Tools for Talking When Stakes Are High" by Kerry Patterson, Joseph Grenny, Ron McMillan, and Al Switzler to enhance your discussion skills and expressing opinions.
Engage in online discussion forums or join conversation groups to practice expressing your viewpoints and actively listening to others.
Participate in mock group discussions with friends or fellow learners, focusing on effective communication and respectful exchange of ideas.

Week 5:

Read "Speak Like Churchill, Stand Like Lincoln: 21 Powerful Secrets of History's Greatest Speakers" by James C. Humes to learn about impromptu speaking and handling Q&A sessions.
Engage in impromptu speaking exercises, such as giving impromptu speeches on random topics.
Prepare and deliver a final presentation, incorporating the skills and techniques learned throughout the course.
